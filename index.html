History of Networks

Networking history can be traced back to the early days of computing when computers were first developed. In the 1950s, the first computer networks were developed for scientific and military purposes, with the goal of sharing resources and information across multiple locations.One of the earliest examples of computer networking was the SAGE (Semi-Automatic Ground Environment) system developed in the 1950s by the United States Air Force. SAGE was a system of interconnected computers used to track and control aircraft.
